I was making breakfast in the kitchen with the supplies I bought on the way over: there wasn’t enough for three so I skipped out on eggs and bacon, watching Ellie and Sylvia eat them from behind a jar of pretzels. The Cardinal hated when we ‘wasted’ her food, as if hoarding jars of canned fruit and beans would add another year to her life in the apocalypse.

“You still have wool on your nose, dummy,” Sylvia said. Ellie laughed, smacking her lips between mouthfuls. An hour earlier, Sylvia awoke me in laughter, untangling a noose of yarn from my neck as she lifted me from the box.
