Background
Start of the project

On Saturday, April 28, 2018, a fatal DUI crash occurred in south Los Angeles. Around 7:30 PM, a drunk driver collided with a silver minivan, and three people were killed in the accidentâ€”an adult, child, and an infant. The only survivors of the crash were Elizabeth and Julian Flores. Each hospitalized in critical condition. Due to the blazing heat, Julian suffered third degree burns and lost nine of his fingers.

Through reference of close friends, their case was brought to the attention of the Quality of Life Plus Student Association at Cal Poly. Club President Harry Koos determined Ryan Kissinger to be the Team Lead of the project on October 14, 2019, after his previous experience as team lead of the ALPS Project (another formal design project.) After receiving around 70 applications, Kissinger narrowed down the team to eight members, representing the fields of Mechanical, Biomedical, and Electrical Engineering. The first meeting was held shortly after three hours of interviews, where the team discussed the ramifications of the project, quickly realizing that the challenge was far beyond just designing a single hand and mirroring the design. Austin Conrad noted that Julian's hands had two distinct levels of disability -- therefore, two approaches would be necessary to complete the project.

The financial constraints of the project only allowed the team $1500 to complete the project. The team rapidly concluded their project would be impossible without additional funding. Team Lead Ryan Kissinger prorposed using a GoFundMe for the project to raise additional funds, and encouraged his team to share the project through their social media. It proved to be incredibly successful: in only two days, the project had over 800 page shares, and had already raised its goal of $5000.

The MIDAS Team

Co-Team Lead of the MIDAS Project, mainly responsible for the left / bionic hand. Assembled the MIDAS team together after being selected for the role by the QL+ Board.
Austin Conrad | Mechanical
Co-Team Lead of the project. Mainly responsible for the right, or non-bionic hand. Exceptional manufacturing knowledge afforded the team guidance on designing parts.
Bryson Chan | Mechanical
Mechanical Engineering student that worked with Austin Conrad on designing the right hand. Modeled finger inserts for the final glove.
Leila Assal | Biomedical

Rachael Peavler | Biomedical
Worked on both the left and right hand teams. Biomedical Engineering student also concentrated in mechanical design. Designed the thumb on the final left hand prosthetic.
Christian Aguirre | Electrical
Electrical Engineering student responsible for circuit design, programming, motor current and voltage testing, and parts selection.
