Turbulent Beginnings

Project Midas was incredibly rapid at the outset. The scope of the project was substantial and heavy: an immense human factor that attracted applicants to give their best efforts to the project. The process began in the fall. I applied as a Team Lead applicant to the Quality of Life Plus Student Association board; under reccomendation from my previous work on the ALPS project, I was selected for this project, handed a stack of nearly seventy resumes, conducting interviews for almost three hours to ensure the proper team was selected for the project.

The team I selected was evenly distributed in abilities, representing a variety of Cal Poly students well-versed in Mechatronics, Biomedical Engineering, and Mechanical Design. The focus of the Mechatronics students was on developing the electronic components, code in either Arduino, Python, or C++ environments, and testing electrical components for satisfactory performance. The Biomedical team focused on developing molds, gathering anthropometric data, and developing biotolerant systems that our challenger's hands could interface with. The Mechanical Design team comprised of developing the solid models and testing the mechanical workings of both hands.
